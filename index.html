About:

In this COVID-19 pandemic, behold the largest online programming competition in the country presented by Notre Dame Information Technology Club. This is our first ever flagship programming contest which will be held every year from now. In memory of Father Richard William Timm, presenting 'Father Timm Memorial Programming Contest' to be held for the first time this October.


Father Timm (March 2, 1923 – September 11, 2020) was one of the founding members of Notre Dame College, Dhaka and our respected 6th principal. Father Timm not only helped Bangladesh by his writing and relief works at the time of the Liberation war, but also was a lifelong friend of Bangladesh contributing to her development in whatever ways he could. Besides his social work, he had an excellent scholarly mind. As a biologist, he had done extensive research works in taxonomy and discovered over 250 species. Father Timm is also the founder of the debate and science club's of Notre Dame College, the first in the country. Through this competition, we will remember him every year for his immense contributions to Science, Notre Dame College, and Bangladesh.
